About the Practice

The surgeon who chose not to do surgery.

Dr. David Mabrie trained as a facial plastic surgeon — residency at Johns Hopkins, fellowship at Stanford. He could have built a surgical practice. He made a deliberate choice not to.

After 25 years of studying what the face responds to, he concluded that injectables — done with the same precision and planning as surgery — could produce better outcomes for more patients, with no downtime, no recovery, and no permanence if preferences change.

He is the only facial plastic surgeon in San Francisco whose practice is focused entirely on non-surgical facial rejuvenation. That focus is not a limitation. It is the reason patients fly in from outside the Bay Area for his appointments.

The Approach

Illumination 360° — the full face as a system.

Most injectable appointments treat the face by area. Forehead this visit. Cheeks next time. It works. But the results can look assembled rather than integrated. The Illumination 360 approach starts differently: Dr. Mabrie maps the full face before touching anything. Every decision accounts for how it changes the rest.

Step 01
Full-Face Assessment
Before any treatment, Dr. Mabrie photographs and analyzes the face from every angle. Symmetry, volume loss, structural support, and the relationship between areas are all mapped.
Step 02
Coordinated Treatment Plan
Treatment priorities are sequenced in the order that produces the most balanced result. A decision about the cheeks affects the midface. Getting the sequence right is what separates a natural result from an obvious one.
Step 03
Ongoing Refinement
Maintenance appointments refine, not restart. Because the full face has been planned, each subsequent visit builds on the existing result rather than addressing isolated areas independently.
Step 04
Patient-Specific Timing
Botox metabolism varies by individual. Dr. Mabrie tracks your personal treatment window across visits so maintenance is timed to your physiology, not a generic schedule.
